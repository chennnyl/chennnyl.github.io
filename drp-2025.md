# The Krein-Milman theorem and elementary results in convex analysis
These are the slides for the talk I gave for my Spring 2025 directed reading program.

I created this presentation under the mentorship of Rémi Berritault based on the text *Fundamentals of Convex Analysis* (Springer, 2001) by Jean-Baptiste Hiriart-Urruty and Claude Lemaréchal. I include it here as evidence of my graduate mathematical experience and because I believe it serves as good introductory material for any undergraduate math students comfortable with linear algebra and some amount of real analysis in one variable.

This talk was put together over the course of a little under a semester and covers material entirely outside the scope of most undergraduate mathematical curricula (although it intersects in its applications in optimization theory with such fields as linear algebra and real analysis). This talk demonstrates my ability to engage in independent exploratory research, work effectively with faculty mentors, and communicate complex mathematical concepts both efficiently and in an easy-to-digest way -- all of which are valuable skills in dense, fast-paced math education environments.
