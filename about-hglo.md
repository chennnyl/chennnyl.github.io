# HGLO101 Final Project
This project can be viewed [here](https://chennnyl.github.io/hglo101). It is a website taking the form of an interactive explainer about data science and demographics produced as my final project for the course HGLO101 (Saving the World with Data), which I took in the fall of 2023 as part of my honors program, then known as Honors Global Communities and now known as Honors Global Challenges and Solutions. The content of this site is largely drawn from the books *8 Billion and Counting* by Jennifer D. Sciubba and *How to Save the World for Just a Trillion Dollars* by Rowan Hooper, and is supplemented with data from the US Census Bureau.

Although the basic scaffolding of the site and the fundamental tools for creating interactive charts are handled by the web framework [Svelte](https://svelte.dev) and the library [`svelte-chartjs`](https://www.npmjs.com/package/svelte-chartjs), I entirely authored the rest of the site, including tooling to parse data from the US Census Bureau, wrangle it into a format agreeable with `svelte-chartjs`, automatically generate an APA-format bibliography, and reactive, on-the-fly chart generation. This project demonstrates my ability to create high-quality visualizations, implement robust systems intercompatible with other libraries, and use digital aids to effectively explain complex topics.
